Privacy

How Growr handles your data

This page explains what Growr stores, what outside services may be used, and what users should expect from the current product.

Plain English App-specific Updated for current build

What Growr collects

Growr may collect account details such as name, email address, password hash, selected plan, saved planner inputs, transaction entries, connected-account summaries, and subscription metadata.

Why the app collects it

That information is used to create accounts, save budgeting data, connect financial accounts, improve recommendations, manage subscriptions, and support password reset or email verification flows.

Connected accounts

If a user links financial accounts, Growr may receive balances, liabilities, transactions, and investment details through Plaid. Coverage depends on the institution and products available through Plaid.

AI features

If Ask Growr is enabled, user questions and relevant saved financial context may be sent to OpenAI to generate educational answers inside the app. Growr should not be used as a substitute for licensed tax, legal, or investment advice.

Email and billing providers

Growr may use third-party providers such as Stripe for billing, Plaid for account linking, OpenAI for AI answers, and an email provider such as Resend for verification and password reset messages.

Data retention and security

Growr aims to store only what is needed to run the service, but no online service can promise perfect security. Users should use strong passwords and protect access to their email account.

Your choices

Users can update profile details, manage billing when Stripe is connected, and choose whether to link external accounts. If you do not want linked financial data used, do not connect those accounts.

Terms

Basic terms for using Growr

These terms are written for the current app and are meant to set straightforward expectations for normal users.

Consumer-friendly Educational product Not financial advice

Educational use only

Growr is an educational budgeting and planning tool. It may surface ideas, forecasts, and suggestions, but it does not provide legal, tax, accounting, or personalized investment advice.

User responsibility

Users are responsible for the accuracy of the information they enter or connect. If figures are incomplete or incorrect, recommendations and forecasts may also be incomplete or incorrect.

Forecasts and assumptions

Investment projections and financial suggestions are estimates built from assumptions. Actual market performance, taxes, fees, rates, and personal circumstances can change outcomes materially.

Accounts and security

Users should keep login credentials private and use a valid email address they control. Growr may require email verification for sensitive actions like linked-account access or billing actions.

Billing

If paid plans are enabled, subscription billing, payment updates, and cancellation may be handled by Stripe or another billing provider. Pricing, trial availability, and access controls may change over time.

Third-party services

Some features depend on outside providers. Growr is not responsible for outages, errors, institution coverage gaps, or policy changes made by Plaid, Stripe, OpenAI, or email providers.

Service changes

Growr may add, remove, pause, or update features over time. Continued use of the app after changes means the user accepts those updated terms.

Growr is built to help everyday people budget, plan, and invest with more clarity.
